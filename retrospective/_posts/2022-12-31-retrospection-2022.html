Be the most backward person

In the book The Way of a Programmer, Ask a Mentor it is written like this.


Fill your surroundings with people who are better than you. Find a team where you will be the most backward person and where there is room for further growth.


This means that you should not just stay on the worst, but consciously find ways to improve and imitate the better until you get to the same level as everyone else. Failure to do so will only put a strain on the team. I think it means to focus on growth instead of being complacent. Of course, it will be hard to follow, but it is an opportunity for us to grow. It always feels good to think about growth, but on the other hand, ask myself, Am I a good person to work with? makes me think about it. If I'm the most backward person, what can they learn from me?

What I felt, you must be ready to leave at any time. Preparations means things like skills, portfolio, and career. And you should record continuously and fulfil self-correction.

Regulating lines


All designers, including architects design unconsciously through regulating lines. This serves as a kind of design guideline. So, regulating lines play a very important role in parametric design. Parametric design is not a statically planned design. This is a design in which all design elements respond to parameters and change state. That is, the parameters are interdependent.

Regulation lines are a tool to make these parametric models more tightly interdependent. I think one of the important abilities that a computational designer should have is designing regulating lines.


Methods for quantitative evaluation of physical space

A computational designer's most powerful weapon is quantitatively evaluating designs. All designs are not planned just for decoration. The current state of design represents for function and purpose and The function and purpose of the design can be expressed as a score against which the plan can be evaluated. God is in debuggerüêû

It's no exaggeration to say that 80% of a computational designer's task is debugging. When I was studying algorithms before getting a job, all I did was print() to figure out where the code had problems. This method of debugging is very unproductive and takes a long time to troubleshoot. However, if you use a debugger, you can set a break point at the point where the problem occurs, so you can see the problem from the point of view of the bug. If you're a computational designer deal with 2D geometry, consider adding a debugvisualizer. it would be very productive.
Architects usually deal with geometry using Rhino's Grasshopper and ghPython. When I didn't know this, I usually worked with ghPython. But unfortunately, Rhino's ghPython doesn't have a debugger. So it's too hard to find where is the problem with my script. If Rhino included this feature, it would be an even more powerful geometry tool.


Test code

Architecture is parametrically intertwined with various elements. (Core, Parking, Mass etc) That's why, when developing a new feature problems may occur at points related to that feature. Test code helps catch these problems early. TDD is the best development method in terms of CI/CD. However, writing tests involving geometry can take longer than developing new features. Also, there are no best practices to know what is the correct geometry testing method.
