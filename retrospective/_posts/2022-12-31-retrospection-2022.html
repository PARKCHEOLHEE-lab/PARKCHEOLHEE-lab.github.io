Thank you 2022

This year felt more special than any other year. I got a job at a company I was interested in, met great people, and learned a lot of new things from them. And also I felt a lot of lack of myself. It's hard to list everything I've felt this year, but I'd like to record some of the most powerful memories.



God is in debugger !

It's no exaggeration to say that 80% of a computational designer's task is debugging. When I was studying algorithms before getting a job, all I did was print() to figure out where the code had problems. This method of debugging is very unproductive and takes a long time to troubleshoot. However, if you use a debugger, you can set a break point at the point where the problem occurs, so you can see the problem from the point of view of the bug. If a debugvisualizer is added to this, the geometry developer becomes very productive.
Architects usually deal with geometry using Rhino's Grasshopper and ghPython. When I didn't know this, I usually worked with ghPython.

But unfortunately, debugger does not exist in ghPython in Rhino.
