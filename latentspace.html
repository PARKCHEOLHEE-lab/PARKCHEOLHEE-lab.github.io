I think the term latent space appropriately encompasses my interests. Here, space is a term that bridges three domains I'm interested in: 1) the latent space of machine learning, where hidden representations within data are encoded; 2) the physical space of architecture, which organizes human experience and behavior; and 3) the design space of optimization, where parametric configurations are explored to satisfy objectives.

Despite their differences, all three can be interpreted through a geometric perspective, allowing them to be integrated into a unified framework for computational design thinking.
