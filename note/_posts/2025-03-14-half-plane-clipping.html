  • Half-Plane Clipping
    • Defining the Half-Plane:
      Given a point \( s \) and a unit vector \( \mathbf{n} \), the half-plane is defined as follows. Where \(s\) is the clipping line's reference point, points satisfying \((\mathbf{x} - s) \cdot \mathbf{n} \le 0\) are inside the half-plane, while those with \((\mathbf{x} - s) \cdot \mathbf{n} > 0\) are outside. \[ \{\, \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \mid (\mathbf{x} - s) \cdot \mathbf{n} \le 0 \} \]

    • Sutherland-Hodgman Algorithm:
      This algorithm clips a polygon against a half-plane by processing each edge and determining intersections.
      • Step 1: Determine if a Point is Inside
        For a vertex \( \mathbf{x} \) of the polygon, check: \[ (\mathbf{x} - s) \cdot \mathbf{n} \le 0 \] If true, \( \mathbf{x} \) is inside the half-plane.
      • Step 2: Edge Intersection
        For an edge with endpoints \( \mathbf{p} \) and \( \mathbf{q} \), parameterize the edge: \[ \mathbf{x}(t) = \mathbf{p} + t(\mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}), \quad t \in [0,1] \] To find the intersection \( \mathbf{x}(t) \) with the half-plane boundary, set: \[ (\mathbf{x}(t) - s) \cdot \mathbf{n} = 0 \] Substituting the parameterization: \[ (\mathbf{p} - s) \cdot \mathbf{n} + t\, (\mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}) \cdot \mathbf{n} = 0 \] Solving for \( t \): \[ t = -\frac{(\mathbf{p} - s) \cdot \mathbf{n}}{(\mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}) \cdot \mathbf{n} + 1 \times 10^{-8}} \] This \( t \) determines the intersection point: \[ \mathbf{x}_{\text{inter}} = \mathbf{p} + t(\mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}) \]
      • Step 3: Clipping the Polygon
        For each polygon edge:
        • If both endpoints are inside, add the endpoint \( \mathbf{q} \) to the output.
        • If one endpoint is inside and the other is outside, add the computed intersection point.
        • If both endpoints are outside, discard the edge.
        The resulting set of vertices forms the clipped polygon.
